Parsons

Lead UX/UI Engineer

Due to the sensitive nature of some projects with the Department of Defense (DoD), I am only allowed to show certain pieces.

User research, user experience, rapid prototyping, mobile applications, agile development, and more, are assorted mechanisms that I performed on daily basis. User experience focuses on the user and identifying problems and solve those problems with a design solution.

Our United States of America Department of Defense utilizes modern solutions for our warfighter and some of those solutions are mission applications. While at Parsons, I was a part of various products but notable applications are C2Core Air and WebTas Enterprise (WE).

Both applications were being used in real-time but needed both user experience updating focusing on the analysts, commanders, and airman.

WebTAS Project

This project was an Air Space Precision Engagement Research and Engineering program through the Air Force that provided various web applications for situational understanding and intelligence analysis. WebTAS helped provided secure integration, analysis, visualization, and actionable information across a suite of WebTAS applications.

Users needed a better way to login to the platform and access the applications while providing seamless usability between applications and updating. Discovery, defining, ideating, and delivering are the steps I took to enhance the user experience and the end product.

Phase 1 & 2: Discovery, Research, and Analysis
Phase 3 & 4: Ideate, Prototype, and Delivery


C2Core

C2Core® it self is a suite of command and control (C2) tools developed for flight operators by operators to be used across multiple domains. Specifically, what I worked on is C2Core® Air. C2Core Air was developed to solve planning miscommunication, mission tasking, and data visualization.

Users needed a better way to control and schedule tasks for gas refuels for flights and tankers.
